Why Now

A rare moment in tech — and MSPs

"The companies that redefine industries didn't win by asking 'how do we do this better?' They won by asking 'what would this look like if it were invented today?'"
— SuperOps Founder's Note, 2026
⚡
AI is rewriting the rules
Manual workflows are being automated. Systems that required constant oversight can now become autonomous. The underlying rules of the MSP industry are shifting — right now.
🏗️
Legacy players can't catch up
ConnectWise, Autotask, Kaseya are adding AI to 15-year-old architecture. SuperOps is building software designed around AI from day one. That's structural — not a claim.
🧠

The future of MSPs won't be defined by vendor announcements. It'll be defined by practitioners who are experimenting, building, and sharing what works — in public.
Community Strategy

A two-month seeding strategy
before the AI Marketplace launches

This isn't a typical vendor community. Ambassadors lead the industry conversation that the AI Marketplace will answer — not the other way around.

Phase 01
Month 1
The Provocation Phase
Ambassadors seed one question across MSP communities: "Is the way MSPs work today actually right — or just the way it's always been done?"

Not as a SuperOps pitch. As a genuine industry debate. Hot takes, workflow teardowns, honest AI experiments.
Goal: spark the conversation, not answer it yet.
Phase 02
Month 2
The Vision Phase
The conversation shifts from "is change coming" to "what does the future look like." Ambassadors describe workflows they wish existed. Problems current tools can't solve.

SuperOps listens and shapes. This becomes the intellectual foundation for launch.
Goal: define the future before the product arrives.
Phase 03
Launch Day
The Marketplace Arrives
By launch day, the community hasn't been sold to — they've been part of building the thinking.

Ambassadors aren't announcing a product. They're introducing the answer to questions they've been asking in public for two months.
Goal: launch into a community that's already primed.

The SUPER Model

How we run the program

A framework focused on your success as an ambassador, and the community's impact on the industry.

S
Source the Right Ambassadors
High-fit practitioners identified using product usage, NPS scores, CSM nominations, and community activity. Focus on MSP owners, engineers, and architects actively experimenting with AI.
AI angle
Prioritize candidates already running AI experiments — not just curious. Their real stories are the raw material for the community strategy.
U
Understand Their Goals
A short survey and 1:1 onboarding call to uncover each ambassador's goals, constraints, and preferred ways to contribute — before asking anything of them.
AI angle
Map each ambassador's AI maturity. Early adopters lead Month 1 provocations. Skeptics-turned-believers are your most powerful Month 2 voices.
P
Pilot with a Small Cohort
Start with 8–10 founding voices. Test tracks, calibrate expectations, validate the program before scaling to broader audiences.
AI angle
The founding cohort seeds the AI conversation for the two-month runway to marketplace launch. Small is intentional — credibility over volume.
E
Engage with Meaningful Tracks
Clear opt-in tracks with a light, predictable quarterly cadence. Designed to respect that you're running an MSP — this isn't a second job.
AI angle
Each track has an AI-specific activity: Technical Architects build on the marketplace. Content Chroniclers publish AI workflow stories. Regional Leads host AI-themed events.
R
Recognize and Refine
Contributions celebrated through spotlights, credentials, perks, and early product access. Ambassador feedback continuously shapes the SuperOps roadmap.
AI angle
Ambassadors who shape the AI Marketplace roadmap get named credit and early access. Their influence is visible and real — not symbolic.

Who Should Apply

We keep the cohort small and curated

Every ambassador has real impact. Here's who should apply and the signals we look for.

✓
MSP operator with hands-on experience
Currently working in IT or MSP as an owner, engineer, technician, or consultant — with real experience running or supporting managed services.
✓
Actively experimenting with AI
Not just curious about AI — testing it, building with it, or evaluating it seriously in your own MSP operations. This is the most important signal we look for.
✓
Active voice in the MSP community
A consistent history contributing to MSP communities — LinkedIn, forums, user groups, events. You contribute, not just consume.
✓
Independent thinker
You've publicly disagreed with industry consensus at least once. We want thinkers, not validators. The founding cohort needs voices people actually listen to.
✓
Community-first mindset
Genuine passion for peer-led learning and helping other MSPs grow. You know the difference between building a community and running a marketing channel.
✓
Able to commit 4–6 hours per month
Across events, content, and feedback sessions. Designed to respect that you're running an MSP — a small investment in leading your industry.
Process

Six steps from application to impact

A clear journey that respects your time and maximizes the value you get from the program.

Step 01
Apply
Short application — your role, experience, and preferred track. No long essays. Just a clear picture of who you are and how you contribute.
Step 02
Review & Select
Rolling review using eligibility criteria and product-usage signals. Small, high-fit founding cohort selected typically within 5 business days.
Step 03
Onboard
Short survey, then a 1:1 call to align on goals and activities. Welcome pack and full program overview included.
Step 04
Engage
Pick your track. Each quarter, participate in events, feedback sessions, and advocacy activities at your own pace.
Step 05
Recognize & Reward
Contributions celebrated through spotlights, credentials, perks, and early product access. Your feedback shapes the SuperOps roadmap.
Step 06
Evolve Together
Quarterly program reviews. Tracks and benefits refined based on what delivers the most value — to ambassadors and to the community.
